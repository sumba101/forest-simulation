WHY THE DISTRIBUTION ISN'T A POWER LAW
=======================================
Date: 2026-04-24

The short answer: our model has too many characteristic scales — the physics sets a
preferred fire size rather than letting any size be equally likely.

1. Lightning rate is far too high relative to tree growth
---------------------------------------------------------
The Drossel-Schwabl model (the theoretical SOC forest fire) requires f/p << 1 —
lightning must be extremely rare compared to tree growth. This timescale separation
is the entire mechanism: forests grow dense and interconnected before lightning
strikes, so when a fire starts it burns through a giant cluster.

Current values: sapling_to_tree = 0.0008, lightning_chance = 0.03.
Per-tick, lightning is ~37x faster than any given sapling becoming a tree. The forest
never accumulates large enough connected clusters for rare catastrophic fires, so all
fires end up medium-sized — producing the hump in the chart, not a power-law tail.

2. Growth pauses during fire
-----------------------------
The classic model runs growth and lightning simultaneously, every tick. The separation
of timescales is emergent, not enforced. Our explicit growth pause during fire breaks
the SOC mechanism: fires can't compete with new growth reaching into adjacent empty
cells while they spread, which changes how cluster boundaries behave.

3. Ignition probability = 0.4, not 1.0
----------------------------------------
The Drossel-Schwabl model uses ignition_chance = 1. Our 40% chance means fire
frequently self-extinguishes before consuming a connected cluster. This introduces a
characteristic patch size (set by the geometric distribution of spread failures),
which peaks the distribution rather than flattening it.

4. Grass as permanent firebreaks
----------------------------------
Grass cells are unburnable and cover a large fraction of the grid. This caps the
maximum connected tree cluster size structurally — not through dynamics, but through
geometry. A hard geometric cap means large fires are impossible regardless of tree
density, cutting off the right tail of the distribution.

5. Multi-tick fuel
-------------------
Real SOC models burn a cell in one step (tree → ash instantly). Our fuel_min=2 /
fuel_max=6 lets a cell burn for up to 7 ticks. This adds a characteristic timescale
to the burning process and a characteristic spatial scale to how far fire spreads
from each cell.

WHAT WOULD GIVE A POWER LAW
=============================
- Set lightning_chance to ~0.0005 (much rarer than growth)
- Remove the growth pause during fire
- Set ignition_chance to 1.0
- Make grass burnable again (or remove grass entirely)
- The dashed τ=1.5 reference line on the log-log chart shows the target slope
